Solar Today in Wisconsin

Wisconsin’s solar industry is booming, with over 3,000 MW of installed capacity in 2025, enough to power approximately 450,000 homes. From rooftop installations in Milwaukee to large-scale solar farms in rural Dane County, solar energy is reducing electricity costs and creating sustainable communities.

3,000 MW

Installed solar capacity in 2025.

450,000 Homes

Powered by solar energy annually.

Did You Know? Milwaukee’s solar installations grew 20% in 2024, driven by urban rooftop projects.

Source: Solar Energy Industries Association (SEIA), 2025

Why It Matters

  • Environmental Impact: Solar power cuts carbon emissions, helping Wisconsin meet its goal of 100% carbon-free electricity by 2050.
  • Economic Benefits: The solar industry supports over 3,000 jobs and attracts millions in investments annually.
  • Energy Independence: Locally produced solar energy reduces reliance on imported fossil fuels, enhancing energy security.

Tip: Community solar programs let renters access solar benefits without installing panels.

Source: Wisconsin Public Service Commission, 2025

How It All Started

2010

Focus on Energy program launches solar rebates, sparking early adoption in Wisconsin.

2015

Wisconsin reaches 100 MW of solar capacity, driven by federal tax credits.

2020

Large-scale solar farms, like Badger Hollow, begin construction, boosting capacity.

2025

Solar capacity hits 3,000 MW, powering 450,000 homes annually.

Quick Fact: The Badger Hollow Solar Farm, completed in 2021, generates 300 MW, enough for 90,000 homes.

Source: Wisconsin Public Service Commission, 2025
