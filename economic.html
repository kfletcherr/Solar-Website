Job Creation

Wisconsin’s solar industry employs over 3,000 workers in roles like panel manufacturing, installation, and maintenance. Projects like the Badger Hollow Solar Farm have created hundreds of local jobs, boosting economies in rural areas like Iowa County.

In 2025, the solar sector added 500 new jobs, a 15% increase from 2024. Training programs, such as those offered by Midwest Renewable Energy Association, are preparing workers for these opportunities.

Tip: Check Midwest Renewable Energy Association for solar job training workshops in Wisconsin.

Source: Solar Jobs Census, 2025

Solar Growth Over Time

Quick Fact: Wisconsin’s solar capacity grew 450% from 2020 to 2025, outpacing many Midwest states.

Source: U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), 2025

Energy Independence

Solar power reduces Wisconsin’s reliance on imported coal and natural gas, with local solar farms generating 3,600 GWh annually. This strengthens grid reliability and keeps energy dollars within the state.

Did You Know? Local solar farms save Wisconsin $100 million annually in avoided fuel imports.

Source: Midwest Renewable Energy Association, 2025
